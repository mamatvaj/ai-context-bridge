Overview

AI Context Bridge is a Chrome extension that acts as a bridge between any webpage and your favourite AI assistant. Instead of manually copying text, switching tabs, and pasting — the extension does it all with a single click.

🪄 Floating Widget

A draggable panel that appears on every page. Select text to trigger it, or click the FAB button in the corner.

💬 Selection Tooltip

A mini action bar pops up instantly above highlighted text — click Explain or Summarize without opening the full panel.

🖱️ Right-Click Menu

Highlight text, right-click, and choose an AI action from the browser context menu.

✨ Custom Prompts

Create up to 10 reusable prompt templates. They appear everywhere — widget, tooltip, and right-click menu.

📄 Full Page Mode

Capture the entire page's text content, not just a selection — useful for summarising articles.

🚀 Auto-Submit

Optionally have the extension click Send automatically after injecting your prompt into the AI platform.

ℹ️ No API keys needed. The extension opens the AI platform's website in a new tab and types your prompt into the text box — exactly as if you did it yourself.

Installation

Load as Unpacked Extension (Developer Mode)

Because this extension is distributed as a ZIP file, you install it in Chrome's developer mode:

  1. 1
    Download & unzip the ai-context-bridge.zip file to a permanent folder on your computer (e.g. C:\Extensions\ai-context-bridge or ~/Extensions/ai-context-bridge). Do not delete this folder after installing.
  2. 2
    Open Chrome and navigate to chrome://extensions
  3. 3
    Enable Developer Mode by toggling the switch in the top-right corner of the extensions page.
  4. 4
    Click "Load unpacked" and select the ai-context-bridge folder (the one containing manifest.json).
  5. 5
    Pin the extension by clicking the puzzle-piece icon in the Chrome toolbar → find "AI Context Bridge" → click the pin icon.
⚠️ Keep the folder. Chrome loads the extension directly from the folder. Moving or deleting it will break the extension. You will need to reload it from the new location.

Verifying Installation

Open any webpage (e.g. a news article). You should see a teal/blue circular FAB button in the bottom-right corner. If you don't see it, make sure "Floating Widget" is enabled in the extension popup.

Quick Start

Get up and running in under 60 seconds:

  1. 1
    Go to any article or webpage — for example a news article, blog post, or Wikipedia page.
  2. 2
    Select some text with your mouse. A mini tooltip appears above the selection with "💡 Explain" and "📝 Summarize" buttons.
  3. 3
    Click "💡 Explain". The extension opens your default AI platform (Claude by default) in a new tab with the prompt pre-filled.
  4. 4
    Hit Send in the AI chat and get your answer. That's it.
💡 Tip: Click the extension icon in the toolbar to change your default AI platform. Your choice is saved automatically — no Save button needed.

Floating Widget

The floating widget is a draggable panel that lives on every webpage. It is your main control centre for sending content to AI platforms.

Opening the Widget

There are two ways to open it:

  • A
    Click the FAB — the circular gradient button in the bottom-right corner of any page.
  • B
    Click "⚡ More…" in the selection tooltip that appears when you highlight text.

Dragging & Repositioning

Click and hold the header bar and drag the panel anywhere on screen. The FAB button is also draggable — click and drag it to move it to a different corner. Positions are remembered within the session.

💡 The widget uses Shadow DOM isolation — it cannot be broken or hidden by the host page's CSS, even on heavily styled sites.

  2. 2

  3. 3
    Click one of the quick actions: 💡 Explain, 📝 Summarize, or ⚡ More…

Enabling / Disabling

The tooltip can be turned off in the extension popup under Behaviour → Selection Tooltip. This is useful if you find it intrusive on certain sites where text selection is part of the UI.

Built-in Actions

Three actions are always available in every entry point (widget, tooltip, right-click menu):

💡 Explain

Wraps the selected text in: "Please explain the following [selected text / full page content] clearly and concisely:" then appends the text. Best for: definitions, technical concepts, passages in a foreign language.

📝 Summarize

Wraps the text in: "Please simplify and summarize the following [selected text / full page content]:" then appends the text. Best for: long articles, reports, meeting notes.

✏️ Custom Ask

In the widget: clicking this button opens a small textarea above the action buttons. Type your own instruction (e.g. "Translate this to French"), then click 🚀 Send. Your instruction is placed before the selected text like: "[Your instruction]\n\nContext:\n[selected text]".

In the right-click menu: sends the raw selected text without any prefix — useful when you want to paste it into an ongoing conversation manually.

💡 With Full Page Mode active, all three actions use the full page text instead of any selection. The prompt prefix still changes based on which button you click.

Custom Prompt Templates

Save your frequently-used AI instructions as reusable templates. Once created, they appear in the floating widget ("My Prompts" section), the right-click context menu, and are accessible from any page.

Creating a Custom Prompt

  1. 1
    Click the extension icon to open the popup.
  2. 2
    Scroll to Prompt Templates at the bottom.
  3. 3
    Click the green + button to the right of the section title.
  4. 4
    Fill in the Template Name — this is what appears in menus and buttons. Supports emoji + text (e.g. "🎯 Rewrite Formally"). Maximum 40 characters.
  5. 5
    Fill in the Prompt Template — the instruction sent to the AI. Maximum 500 characters. Your selected text is appended automatically after this prompt.
  6. 6
    Click Save Template. The prompt is immediately available everywhere.

How Custom Prompts Work

When you trigger a custom prompt, the extension sends:

[Your Prompt Template]

[Selected text or full page content]

Example: if your template is "Translate the following to Hindi:" and you select "Hello, how are you?", the AI receives: "Translate the following to Hindi:\n\nHello, how are you?"

Deleting a Custom Prompt

In the popup, each custom prompt chip has a small × button. Clicking it removes the template immediately from all locations (widget, right-click menu).

Full Page Mode

Instead of sending highlighted text, Full Page Mode captures the entire visible text of the current webpage and sends that to the AI platform.

Enabling Full Page Mode

In the floating widget, click the "Full Page" button in the segmented toggle at the top of the panel. The toggle highlights to show it is active. The preview box updates to show the first 200 characters of the page content.

When to Use Full Page Mode

ScenarioUse case
Summarise an articleEnable Full Page + Summarize to get a summary of the entire page
Extract key factsEnable Full Page + Custom Prompt → "List the 5 key facts from this page"
Translate a pageEnable Full Page + Custom Prompt → "Translate this to [language]"
Analyse a reportEnable Full Page + Explain for a breakdown of a complex document
⚠️ Character limit: Full Page content is capped at 50,000 characters to avoid hitting AI platform input limits. Very long pages are truncated at this point.
ℹ️ Full Page Mode captures document.body.innerText — this is the human-readable text of the page, excluding HTML tags, scripts, and hidden elements.

Enabling Auto-Submit

Open the extension popup → Behaviour → Auto-Submit toggle. The setting is saved immediately.

Troubleshooting

FAB button is not visible

1. Open the extension popup and check that Floating Widget is toggled ON.
2. Refresh the page (the widget injects on page load).
3. Check chrome://extensions to ensure the extension is enabled and not showing an error.

Text is not being injected into the AI platform

1. Make sure you are logged in to the AI platform in your browser.
2. The platform may have updated its UI. The extension retries injection 25–30 times with delays — wait up to 5 seconds after the tab opens.
3. If using Auto-Submit, try disabling it and pressing Send manually to see if the text injected successfully.
4. Try reloading the extension at chrome://extensions and retrying.

Only part of the prompt appears (e.g. only the first line)

This was a known issue with Gemini's Quill editor clipboard interceptor. Fixed in v1.20 by using execCommand("insertText") instead of ClipboardEvent simulation. If it recurs, try reloading the extension.

Meta AI / Gemini not available in platform selector

Gemini and Meta AI have been temporarily removed from the platform selector due to injection instability caused by recent UI changes on those platforms. Their injector code is preserved and will be re-enabled in a future update once selectors are fixed. Use Claude, ChatGPT, or Grok in the meantime.

Auto-Submit does not fire

1. The AI platform may be slow to load — the Send button needs to be enabled before Auto-Submit can click it. Allow up to 6 seconds.
2. Some platforms disable the Send button until their React/Angular state validates the input. Try typing a character in the input box to trigger validation, then press Enter.
3. Try disabling Auto-Submit and sending manually.

The widget causes visual issues on a specific site

The widget uses Shadow DOM which should prevent CSS conflicts. If you see issues, try toggling the Floating Widget off for that session from the popup. Please report the URL so it can be investigated.

Custom prompts are not showing in the right-click menu

The context menu is rebuilt dynamically when prompts change. If it does not update:
1. Wait 2–3 seconds and right-click again.
2. Reload the extension at chrome://extensions.
3. Ensure chrome.contextMenus permission is granted (it should be by default).

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this extension send my data anywhere?

No. The extension stores your settings and custom prompts locally in Chrome's chrome.storage.local — they never leave your browser. The only "data" that goes anywhere is what you explicitly send to AI platforms when you use the extension, which goes directly to the AI platform's website in a new tab — exactly as if you typed it yourself.

Do I need API keys?

No. The extension uses the AI platforms' standard web interfaces, not their APIs. No accounts, no billing, no keys needed beyond the free account you already have with each platform.

Why does the tab open but nothing is typed?

You are most likely not logged in to that AI platform. Log in and try again. The extension cannot proceed past a login wall.

Can I use this on any website?

Yes — the extension runs on all websites (<all_urls>). The only exceptions are the AI platform pages themselves (claude.ai, chatgpt.com, etc.) where the extension's widget is intentionally excluded.

Can I change the keyboard shortcut?

Currently there is no keyboard shortcut. You can add one via Chrome's extension shortcut settings at chrome://extensions/shortcuts, but this only controls opening the popup. The widget is opened by clicking the FAB or from the selection tooltip.

Will the extension break when AI platforms update their UI?

Possibly — the injectors rely on CSS selectors and DOM structure that can change when platforms update. When this happens the text injection or auto-submit may stop working until the extension is updated. Check the creator's blog for updates.

What is the maximum number of custom prompts?

10 custom prompt templates maximum. Each name can be up to 40 characters and each prompt body up to 500 characters.

How do I uninstall?

Go to chrome://extensions, find "AI Context Bridge", and click Remove. All locally stored settings and custom prompts will be deleted.
